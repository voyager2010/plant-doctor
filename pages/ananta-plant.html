Ananta Plant

🌿 Ananta Plant (Anantmool)- (Basic Info)

Common Name: Ananta Plant / Anantmool
Scientific Name: Hemidesmus indicus
Family: Apocynaceae
Origin: India and Southeast Asia

🌿 Detailed Description

Scientific Name: Hemidesmus indicus

The Ananta Plant, also known as Anantmool, is a medicinal climbing plant commonly found in India. It grows along the ground or climbs with support using its long stems.

The leaves are small, green, and oval-shaped. The plant produces small flowers and has long, aromatic roots which are highly valued.

The roots of this plant are widely used in Ayurveda for their cooling and healing properties. It helps purify blood, improve digestion, and treat skin problems.

This plant grows well in warm climates with moderate sunlight and well-drained soil. It is easy to grow and maintain.

Due to its medicinal importance and natural benefits, the Ananta plant is widely used in herbal medicine and home remedies.

🌱 Care Guide

  • Water: Regular watering
  • Light: Partial to full sunlight
  • Temperature: 20°C – 35°C
  • Soil: Well-draining soil

🌿 Uses

  • Used in Ayurvedic medicine
  • Helps purify blood
  • Treats skin problems
  • Used in herbal drinks

🌱 Propagation

  1. Grow using seeds or root cuttings
  2. Plant in warm climate

⚠️ Common Problems

  • Overwatering causes root rot
  • Needs enough sunlight for growth

🌼 Benefits

  • Medicinal uses
  • Cooling effect on body
  • Easy to grow
